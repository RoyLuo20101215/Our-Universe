Moons & Satellites

Introduction

Moons and natural satellites serve as essential companions to planets, orbiting them in dynamic systems that reveal the complexities of celestial mechanics and planetary formation. These varied worlds—some with icy crusts and hidden oceans, others cloaked in volcanic activity or thick atmospheres—offer unique insights into the evolution of the solar system and beyond. Each moon and satellite tells a story shaped by its interactions with its parent planet, gravity, and space itself. Together, they present a fascinating realm for exploration, uncovering mysteries of geological activity, potential habitability, and the forces that govern celestial bodies.
